NORTH is a model-agnostic layer that helps AI systems think more carefully before responding. It evaluates prompts through a structured set of human knowledge systems — governance, science, philosophy, engineering and culture — applying a triadic constraint lens (I/R/Sem), a balanced five-note chord of perspectives, and a torsion check to reduce drift, overshoot, and blind agreement.

Rather than replacing a model, NORTH sits between intent and output — adding tension, reflection, and structural accountability. It can work with different models and providers, acting as a navigational compass for responsible thought and handling.
